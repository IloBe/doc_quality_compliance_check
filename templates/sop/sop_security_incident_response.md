---

## 1. Purpose

Define the process to detect, triage, contain, eradicate, recover from, and learn from security incidents.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to incidents affecting confidentiality, integrity, availability, or compliance evidence.

---

## 3. Incident Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Typical Response Time |
|------|-------------|-----------------------|
| Sev 1 | Critical business/security impact | Immediate |
| Sev 2 | High impact, partial service degradation | Urgent |
| Sev 3 | Limited/localized impact | Standard |

---

## 4. Response Lifecycle

1. Detection and reporting.
2. Triage and severity classification.
3. Containment.
4. Eradication and remediation.
5. Recovery and service validation.
6. Post-incident review and CAPA.

---

## 5. Roles and Communication

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Incident Commander | Coordinates response |
| Security Lead | Technical investigation and containment |
| Engineering Lead | Recovery and remediation execution |
| Compliance/Legal | Notification assessment and regulatory liaison |

---

## 6. Notification and Escalation

- Define internal escalation matrix by severity.
- Assess external notification obligations (contractual/regulatory).
- Maintain timestamped communication log.

---

## 7. Evidence Handling

- Preserve logs and forensic artifacts.
- Maintain chain-of-custody where required.
- Record actions and decisions for auditability.

---

## 8. Post-Incident Activities

| Activity | Output |
|----------|--------|
| Root cause analysis | Incident report |
| Corrective/preventive actions | CAPA records |
| Control updates | Updated SOPs/configurations |
